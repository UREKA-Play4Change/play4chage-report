The growth of smart cities requires citizens prepared to understand and engage with the digital and environmental transformations that characterise them, in alignment with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals 11 and~13. In this context, the U!REKA mission promotes the transition towards more sustainable and intelligent cities, identifying citizens' digital literacy as a strategic vector for change. This work proposes \textit{Play4Change}, an adaptive and gamified learning platform designed for citizens, integrating an artificial intelligence agent capable of generating educational content, detecting learning difficulties in real time, and proposing personalised alternative learning paths. The platform comprises a mobile client, a web administration portal, and a stateless server. Learning is structured into progressive micro-competencies, recognised through badges. The adaptive paths generated are reused for citizens with similar difficulty profiles, enhancing the collective effectiveness of the system.
